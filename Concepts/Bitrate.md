# Bitrate

- The *bit rate* (or *bitrate*) is the **number of bits processed per unit of time**
- The *bitrate* is expressed as **bits per second** (bit/s)
	- $1$ kbit/s = $1000$ bit/s
	- $1$ Mbit/s = $1000$ kbit/s

## Multimedia

In multimedia, the bitrate represents the *amount of information* (or *detail*) stored per unit of time of a recording.

The bitrate depends on multiple factors:

- Sample rate
- Sample size
- Encoding/decoding scheme
- Compression algorithms

GOAL: Choose factors above to achieve *trade-off* between *minimizing the bitrate* and *maximizing the information quality*.

When using *lossy compression* for visual or audio data, compression artifacts can become noticeable which will in turn affect the perceived quality.

### Video

Bitrate examples:

- 750 kbit/s: YouTube [[360p]] videos
- 2.5 Mbit/s: YouTube [[720p]] videos
- 3-8 Mbit/s: YouTube [[1080p]] videos

NOTE: Examples above are using [[Advanced Video Coding (H.264)]]
