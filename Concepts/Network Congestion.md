# Network Congestion

*Network congestion* is the reduced [[Quality of Service (QoS)]] that occurs when a *network node or link is carrying or processing more than its capacity*.

Typical effects:

- Queueing delay
- [[Packet Loss]]

- Incremental increase in network load can lead to marginal throughput increases or decreases
- Aggressive retransmissions to compensate for packet loss can increase congestion
- Network congestion can eventually lead to congestive collapse
- Networks use [[Congestion Control]] and [[Congestion Avoidance]] to prevent collapse
