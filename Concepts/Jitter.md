# Jitter

Jitter is the *deviation from true periodicity*, often in relation to a reference clock signal. Jitter is an undesired factor in design of almost all communication links. Specifically in network communication, jitter is the variation in time for data packets to arrive.

Jitter may be caused by *electromagnetic interference* and *crosstalk* with carriers of other signals. Jitter can cause display monitors to flicker, affect processor performance, introduce clicks in audio signals, and loss of transmitted data between network devices. Specifically in network communication, high jitter causes choppy VoIP calls, video stutters, and lag.
