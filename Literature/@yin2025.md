## Notes

Asynchronous uplink/downlink frequencies between ground stations and satellites:

- Using different frequency for uplink and downlink is standard technique *Frequency Division Duplexing*
- Paired with physical asymmetry: uplink always higher than downlink
- Consequences of asymmetry:
	- [[Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR)]] is unequal in both directions
	- Uplink bandwidth usually wider than downlink
	- Throughput (core bottleneck for live migration)
		- Bounded by [[Shannon-Hartley Theorem]]
		- Uplink: High throughput
		- Downlink: Low throughput

## Orbital Edge Computing (OEC)

### Concept of OEC

- Computational tasks directly on satellites, sending data back to Earth for further processing
- Satellites equipped with computing resources, connect to build OEC networks
	- Can offload services for ground and airborne users
	- Satellites and IoT devices can offload computing tasks to OEC networks to achieve:
		- high-speed
		- low-latency
		- low-cost
- Traditionally, satellites are based on the *bent-pipe* architecture:
	- They merely act as relay nodes to transmit data or sensor nodes to receive instructions from ground and send data back to ground
		- In large constellations, this architecture leads to
			- high latency
			- complex routing
			- other complications
- In OEC architecture, satellites are equipped with computational capabilities
	- Can complete tasks as edge servers in network
	- Improves communication performance
	- Enables diversified services
		- In-orbit data preprocessing
		- Real-time remote sensing and image analysis
		- Disaster monitoring

### Satellite Constellations, Networking and Communication

#### Satellite Constellation

- System of artificial satellites with similar functions and parameters
- The satellites can be produced and launched in batches which lowers costs
- They offer unique communication and coverage performance that varies according to the orbital altitude
	- Distinguish between several types
		- [[Low-Earth Orbit (LEO)]]
		- HEO
		- MEO
		- [[Geostationary-Earth Orbit (GEO)]]
- *Inclination*: Angle between the orbital plane and the equatorial plane
	- LEO:
		- 80-100 degrees near-polar orbit
		- 30-60 degrees inclined orbit
- *Altitude*: Distance from satellite in orbit to Earth's surface
	- LEO: 300-1500km
- *Period*: Time it takes for a satellite to orbit the Earth once
	- LEO: 1.4-2.5h
- *Revisit period*: Time it takes for a satellite to pass over the same position on the Earth's surface
- *Number of satellites*: General number of satellites deployed in a satellite constellation
	- LEO: in the range of thousands
- *Coverage*: Observable range of constellation on Earth
	- LEO: global
- *Latency*: Transmission time of one-way communication of satellite to GS
	- LEO: 5-35ms
	- GEO: 270ms
- *Pass duration*: Time interval required for an individual satellite operating in a specific orbit to traverse communication range of a GS
	- LEO: ~10m
- LEO examples: Starlink, Kuiper, Iridium, O3B

- GEO satellite pros/cons:
	- Wide-coverage -> global communication with just three satellites
	- High-altitude and orbit requirement around equator result in high latency

#### Satellite Communications

- Frequency crucial factor for satellite communications
	- Microwave (300 MHz to 300 GHz) suitable because
		- wide bandwidth
		- high freqeuncies
		- ability to use small antennas
	- Most satellites work in C frequency band:
		- ~ 6GHz uplink frequency
		- ~ 4GHz downlink frequency
- Transmission losses include:
	- Free-space path loss (vast majority of total transmission loss)
	- Antenna misalignment loss
		- Imperfections in antenna alignments between transmitter and receiver
	- Polarization loss
		- Misalignment between incoming wave polarization and receiving antenna
	- In ground-to-satellite communication, additionally:
		- Atmospheric absorption loss
			- Interactions of free ions in the ionosphere
			- Absorption and scattering of electromagnetic waves
		- Path loss in urban environments
			- Propagation of electromagnetic waves at low altitudes
			- Shadow fading because of buildings and mountains
- [[Doppler Effect|Doppler]] frequency also poses significant risk to efficacy of satellite systems
- Inter-satellite communication must be considered for
	- Computational offloading
	- Federated learning
	- Resource allocation
- Inter-Satellite Links (ISLs) are divided into:
	- intra-orbit ISLs: connect satellites from the same orbital plane
	- inter-orbit ISLs: connect satellites across orbital planes

#### Satellite Network Topology Modeling

- Satellite networks are dynamic networks composed of nodes and links
	- Nodes are satellites
	- Links are communication links between satellites
- Read more about modeling of topology in paper

### Mobile Computing for IoT: From Ground to Space

- Applications including AR/VR, HD video streaming, autonomous driving require extremely low latency or enormous computational ability
	- Cloud computing not enough to support these applications -> edge computing
	- However, edge computing has limited coverage, especially in remote areas
- Space-air-ground integrated networks are promising for IoT
 - OEC can reduce ground infrastructure and edge processing latency
 - Edge computing can be implemented on virtualized platforms
	 - allows high bandwidth and low latency
	 - decreases privacy and security risks

### Orbital Edge Computing versus Terrestrial Mobile Edge Computing

#### Architecture

- OEC doesn't just transmit raw data back to ground, satellites can provide computing resources

#### Computing

- Software and hardware on satellite systems completely different than on ground computing devices
	- Constrained by volume, weight of satellites and of harsh space environments
- Satellite hardware must be more reliable because of challenging space conditions

#### Control and Management

- Advantage of satellite communication
	- Ubiquitous connection
	- Low latency
	- e.g. New York - London 76ms RTT, optical fiber reduces to 55ms, using ISLs reduces to 50ms
- Establishing connection links on satellites requires minutes of prior attitude adjustment for antenna alignment
	- Need to design instructions for task scheduling
- Energy consumption is important in OEC
	- Distance between electromagnetic waves for satellite communication exceeds hundreds of kilometers which consumes more energy
	- Less of a concern in MEC

#### Mobility

- MEC is troubled by random movements of ground users
	- Challenge of ubiquitous and reliable service connections
- Challenge in OEC is high-speed satellite movement and unstable connection
	- Completely different from MEC

#### Application

- OEC important supplement for MEC
- OEC provides access to computational services in remote and disaster areas
- OEC also offers in-orbit computational services
		- e.g. for remote sensing images

### Advanced Technology in Space

Describes recent advancements in technology that could be beneficial to the organization of OEC.

#### Laser Communication

- Free-Space Optical (FSO) communication offers high-speed, high-capacity data transmission
- FSO still suffers from:
	- Imperfect propagation paths
		- Gases and aerosols can cause absorption and scattering of light
	- Atmospheric turbulence
		- Temperature and pressure fluctuations can influence light trajectory
	- Background noise
	- Pointing loss

#### Massive MIMO

- Massive Multiple Input Multiple Output (MIMO): Technology that uses large number of antennas at transmitter and receiver to improve communication
- Promises to meet 6G requirements
- Massive MIMO enables simultaneous data transmission to multiple users in the same frequency band
	- allows highly focused beamforming
	- concentrates signal energy towards specific users
	- reduces interference
	- improves [[Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR)]] rates
- Challenges:
	- Dynamic topology
	- Synchronization
	- Overhead

#### Smart Satellite

- Satellites typically developed for specific missions
- Hardware and software are tightly coupled, system is inflexible and non-universal
- Software-Defined Satellite (SDS) has been proposed to improve efficiency by using software-defined radio as its universal hardware platform and defining functions via software
- Smart satellites have the following characteristics:
	- Small
	- Energy efficient
	- Hybrid-processor architectures
	- Generalized OS
	- Lightweight virtualized resource management

### Systems

#### Satellite Hardware

- Satellites are often exposed to high-intensity radiation, categorized into:
	- long-term cumulative effects
	- short-term transient effects
- These effects have potential to induce software malfunctions or hardware failures
- Fault tolerance techniques are used to circumvent this
- Challenge of OEC is to provide high-performance and reliable computing services with limited resources
	- Commercial off-the-shelf hardware is more feasible for OEC
		- offers superior performance
		- normally have hybrid architecture

#### Satellite Software

- Traditionally written in low-level languages like Ada, C, C++
- An OS deployed on terrestrial computers is unsuitable for direct application for satellites due to harsh environments
- Reliability and stability are primary considerations for satellite system design
- Increased use of Linux on satellites
	- e.g. SpaceX has custom Linux distro
- Next generation satellite OS show following characteristics:
	- Compatibility
	- Upgradeability
	- AI-supported

#### Architecture

Paper describes three layer architecture composed of LEO, GEO, and GSs (ground stations)

##### Cloud

- GSs can play of ground-based cloud, functions include
	- resource orchestration
	- mobility management
	- access control
	- task scheduling
- GSs can access terrestrial Internet and send computing tasks to data centers
- GSs can also collect telemetry data from satellites to monitor operating status

##### Edge

Hu et al. (93) divide OEC architecture into 4 layers:

- Hardware
	- Physical devices (communication, sensors, computing, storage)
- Virtualization
	- Abstracts physical devices through virtualization and containerization
- Service
	- Isolates tasks and resources through VMs
- Control
	- Regularly updates collected orbit information and synchronizes it with the cloud

##### End

- *End* is the entity being served, origin of the computational tasks or data

### Applications

#### Cases of OEC Applications

- OEC can provide ubiquitous computing power access services
- OEC can provide task scheduling and offloading in space
- OEC has improved efficiency of remote sensing applications by reducing raw data transmission to ground
	- e.g. remote sensing satellite produces 10TB data per day
	- e.g. a weather satellite creates 760GB data per day
- Existing OEC systems leverage DL, RL
- OEC has other applications:
	- Space intelligence
	- VR/AR
	- autonomous vehicles

The paper categorizes the applications by location of data source:

#### Source of Data is Ground

- Satellites can serve as computing or relay nodes to process data received from ground
- GS schedules missions and sends satellite instructions
- Applications in this scenario are *latency-sensitive*

##### AR/VR and Ultra-HD Video Streaming

- Challenges include:
	- Complexity of computations (encoding/decoding, rendering...)
- Terrestrial edge can move the computing pressure to edge
	- However fails when it comes to ubiquitous coverage
- Celestial (19) showed that video conference calls over satellite networks can improve [[Quality of Service (QoS)]] for clients in latency-sensitive applications

##### Internet of Vehicles and Intelligent Traffic

- Satellites are a vital element in providing positioning and navigation services for vehicles

#### Source of Data is Space

- Satellites can serve as data source and can participate in joint training/inference of models through ISLs
- GSs can send models or instructions to satellites
- Advantage: Avoid extensive data transmission between satellites and GSs
	- Saves on communication costs

##### Earth Observation (EO)

- Satellites are essential for capturing earth data (e.g. weather, vegetation, urban development...)
- Data generated is growing in size, causes immense pressure on space-to-ground communication
- OEC can analyze the data, provide near real-time analysis, and enhance decision-making
	- e.g. real-time monitoring of natural disasters
	- can improve response times
- OEC can also filter data before sending it to GS, reducing data transmission

##### Scientific Research

- Scientific experiment satellites are used to gather large amounts of data for scientific research
- Again, transmission via satellite-to-ground downlink is inefficient
	- OEC can help reduce these data transmission requirements

#### Satellite Federated Learning

- Federated learning addresses challenges associated with *centralized data processing* and *privacy concerns* of traditional ML methods
- Satellite federated learning combines satellite technology with ML
- Can differentiate between three architectures:
	- Satellite FL without ISL
	- Satellite FL with ISL
	- GEO or aerial network as parameter server

### Issues, Challenges, and Future Research Directions

#### Issues and Challenges

##### Limited Orbital Resources

- Main energy source comes from solar power
- During flight, satellites can't obtain energy half the time since they're obscured by Earth's shadow
	- Have to rely on own energy storage
		- However, making satellites increasingly smaller limits their energy storage

##### Harsh Environment in Space

- Harsh environments of space poses a challenge for software/hardware designs of satellites

##### Launch Cost Limitations of Satellite Hardware

- HPC hardware:
	- More vulnerable to radiation
	- Higher energy consumption
	- Higher thermal power dissipation
- In space, heat can only be dissipated by radiation after conduction to surface (convection is not possible)
	- Must add:
		- radiation shielding
		- heat dissipation equipment
		- more energy storage
	- This increases the weight significantly, increasing launch costs
		- e.g. SpaceX Falcon 9 launch to deliver a 22800kg LEO satellite costs $62 million
			- $2720 per kg

##### Increased Density of Satellites in Orbit

- LEO networks become increasingly larger in scale
	- e.g. Starlink has more than 3558 satellites in orbit
	- Optimizing computing and communication in large constellations is difficult

##### Managing High Mobility

- High mobility of LEO satellites causes connections between satellites and ground stations to constantly change
	- Leads to challenges of mobility management
- Users aren't even limited to stationary ground stations:
	- Personal terminals
	- Aircrafts
	- Ships

##### Non-Ideal Simulators

- At present, OEC restricted to ground simulation
- Lacking real public constellation telemetry data for OEC simulation
- [[@wang2021|Tiansuan Constellation]] aims to create a computing platform in space for research
- Experiments on real satellites remain expensive

##### Unclear Mechanisms and Structures

- OEC has only few studies on real satellites
- Lack of design for OEC architecture and fully functional constellation
	- Task distribution
	- Collaboration between different satellite types
		- Communication
		- Remote sensing
		- Navigation
	- Systematic description and design of constellation and network topology for OEC

##### Security

- Openness of electromagnetic environment in OEC brings security problems
- Satellite communication channels are vulnerable to
	- interception
	- eavesdropping
	- jamming
- OEC systems require the following capabilities:
	- threat detection
	- threat defense
	- data privacy preservation
- Following emerging technologies are promising solutions:
	- Federated learning
	- Semantic communication
	- Quantum cryptography

#### Opportunities and Future Research

##### LEO Satellite Network Design

- LEO satellite constellations are critical to future development of 6G networks
- Design of satellite constellations for OEC not yet discussed
	- e.g. Starlink is an ISP, doesn't provide OEC services

##### Resource Optimization

- Limited bandwidth and processing capacity impact performance of OEC
- Selecting appropriate routing strategy and node selection is a key challenge
	- Requires considering time-varying nature of network, link and node failure, spatial changes

##### Space Data Center

- Migrating storage closer to user can reduce latency and energy consumption
- Satellites generate massive amounts of data
	- Construction of satellite data center could avoid large amounts of data transmission

##### Satellite Caching Strategies

- Promising method to use satellite caching to improve efficiency of content distribution
- Because of dynamic topology, traditional caching and allocation schemes are not suitable
- Cooperative caching is a research area

##### Computation Hardware and Server Design of OEC

- Designing OEC hardware with energy efficiency and reliability is crucial
- OEC server design is completely different from edge computing servers on ground
- Killing factors of servers are weight and volume
- Harsh space environments lead to hardening and redundancy techniques

##### Virtualization

- Lightweight virtualization was proposed for OEC
- We need a virtualization tool for OEC that can be applied to real satellites

##### Containerization

- Promising technology to package software applications with dependencies into lightweight, portable containers

##### Microservices

- Self-contained, lightweight, and portable attributes make microservices perfect for satellite networks
- Microservice architecture can promote:
	- task migration
	- computation offloading
	- ground-station detection
	- satellite management

##### Resilient Network Slicing

- Virtualization technique where infrastructure is shared by multiple tenants to serve multiple service classes simultaneous

##### Federated Learning

- Important method for computational privacy protection
- Challenge of satellite FL:
	- training rounds may receive data from different clients
	- quality of data is unpredictable

##### Testbed

- OEC only a concept
- Only one testbed on OEC has been proposed: *Celestial* by Pfandzelter
- OEC testbeds should have the following characteristics:
	- Accurate satellite constellation simulation
	- Excellent platform compatibility
	- Customizable resource management
	- Support for actual task execution
