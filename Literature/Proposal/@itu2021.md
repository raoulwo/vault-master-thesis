# @itu2021

Cloud VR is a new cloud computing technology for VR services. VR contents are rendered in the cloud and transmitted via high-quality transport networks (high bandwidth, low latency).

Cloud VR services are divided into two categories based on interaction characteristics:

1. *Weak-interaction* VR services (e.g. VR video services)
2. *Strong-interaction* VR services (e.g. VR games)

Cloud VR service experience is divided into *four phases*:

| Experience Phase                       | Typical Content | Typical Terminal Screen Resolution |
|----------------------------------------|-----------------|------------------------------------|
| **Fair Experience Phase (FEP)**        | 4K              | 2K                                 |
| **Comfortable Experience Phase (CEP)** | 8K              | 4K                                 |
| **Ideal Experience Phase (IEP)**       | 12K             | 8K                                 |
| **Ultimate Experience Phase (UEP)**    | 24K             | 16K                                |


## Network Packet Loss Requirements of Cloud VR

- Cloud VR services typically use [[Transmission Control Protocol (TCP)]] as transmission protocol
	- Packet loss reduces TCP throughput and causes frame freezing
- Strong-interaction and live video services use [[User Datagram Protocol (UDP)]] as transmission protocol
	- Higher packet loss than TCP, potentially causing problems such as erratic display and black screen
